HLTC

Interdisciplinary Certificate Program in Human Language Technology


The University of Colorado now offers an Interdisciplinary Certificate in Human Language Technology designed to provide a rich and broad background for students interested in computational tools for human language processing. This educational program is offered through the joint efforts of the Center for Computational Language and Education Research, the Department of Computer Science, the Department of Linguistics, the Department of Speech, Language and Hearing Sciences, and the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering.


The Human Language Technology Program offers a Masters-level certificate, which accompanies a normal M.A. or M.S. degrees in one of the four Human Language Technology disciplines:

  • Computer Science
  • Electrical and Computer Engineering
  • Linguistics
  • Speech, Language, and Hearing Sciences

Human language technology is an interdisciplinary field that includes key technological and scientific areas like :

  • Computer Speech Recognition and Understanding
  • Speech Modeling, Analysis and Processing for Recognition
  • Natural Language Understanding and Generation
  • Text and Audio-based Information Retrieval
  • Web-based Dialog Agents

For more information about the courses and instructors of this program, please visit the certificate program's web site.


The Certificate in Human Language Technology is designed to promote an interdisciplinary experience for students seeking graduate studies in the fields of speech processing and language technology. As such, HLT Certificates are awarded to students who fullfill the 5 course requirements summarized at the HLT web site.


The HLT Certificate program was established by CSLR an approved by the University of Colorado Boulder to help promote an interdisciplinary experience for students seeking advanced degrees in Linguistics, Electrical Engineering, Computer Science, and Speech, Language and Hearing Sciences. Here, we summarize the students who have fullfilled the requirements and have earned the HLT Certificate. Each student receives their graduate degree from their home department, and the HLT Certificate is coordinated between the four participating departments and CSLR.
